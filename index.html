The Decision

Two paths. Same destination. Very different cost of ownership.

Every vendor here will deliver a working site. The real question is what we own when the dust settles, who picks up the phone at 11pm when something breaks, and whether we are paying a premium for a black box.

⚠ Outsource Path
Pay a vendor. Wait for delivery. Hope it works.
AED55K – 175K
Range across the four design + development quotes received (excl. ongoing retainers)
  • IP and source code transfer is contractual, not cultural. Tribal knowledge stays with the vendor.
  • Every change request after launch is a billable hour. AED 200/hr at the cheapest, AED 500/hr at the high end.
  • Roadmap velocity decoupled from our calendar. Vendor priorities shift without notice.
  • StarBerry option locks us into a 24-month hosting contract. AED 16K+ baked in before we even ship.
  • Three of four vendors based outside the UAE. Time-zone friction, RERA literacy gaps, slower escalation.
★ In-House Build
Use the team we already pay. Ship faster. Own everything.
AED22K
Tooling, cloud, fonts, contingency. Salaries are sunk cost.
  • Source code, design system, and CMS schema live in our GitHub from day one. Zero handover risk.
  • Same team that builds it, supports it. Bug fix turnaround in hours, not vendor SLA tickets.
  • Roadmap is ours. A/B tests, RERA updates, agent onboarding flows ship when we say they ship.
  • Capability we keep forever. The next site, the broker portal, the off-plan microsite, all in-house.
  • This dashboard you are reading is exhibit A. Built in < 1 week by the same team that will build the site.

Internal Digital & Tech team AED 22K all-in 14 weeks
★ Recommended
✓Key Strengths
  • 88% saving versus the highest vendor quote, with full IP retention forever
  • Source code, design system, and CMS schemas live in our GitHub from day one
  • Same team builds and supports. Bug-fix turnaround in hours, not vendor SLA tickets

PX
PIXL Global
Dubai · JLT AED 145K / 152K w/VAT 16–18 weeks
Premium · Most Expensive
✓Key Strengths
  • Strongest real-estate portfolio of all four (Omniyat, Eywa, OBG, Allegiance, Sukoon, Sakani)
  • JLT-based. Same neighbourhood. In-person reviews trivial
  • Highest technical rigour: Next.js 14 SSR, TypeScript 5.5, Zod RERA validation, Lighthouse CI enforcement
  • Native Arabic from day one, not a mirrored layout
  • 90-day warranty plus 30-day post-launch support and bug fixes
  • Granular feature pricing — we can opt out individually if budget tightens
  • Full IP and source-code transfer on completion
!Red Flags
  • By far the most expensive. AED 152K incl. VAT, 175K with optional add-ons
  • Arabic charged as a AED 6,500 add-on — should be standard for any MENA agency
  • 75% upfront payment, 25% on handover. Heavy front-load, weak vendor accountability
  • 16–18 weeks — the longest delivery timeline of the four
  • Platform subscriptions excluded: Vercel, Mapbox, SendGrid, AWS = ~USD 200–450 per month, paid by us
  • Optional retainer of AED 10K/month afterwards, additional hours at AED 200/hr
  • Acknowledges our internal demo build but explicitly treats it as "reference only", not a starting codebase
Bottom line
You get what you pay for, but we are paying for excellence we can replicate internally. Best vendor in the field on technical merit. Worst on price. Choose only if internal capacity is genuinely zero.
SB
StarBerry
UK · Derby AED 78K + AED 75K (24mo) 5–6 months
⚠ Hidden Lock-in
✓Key Strengths
  • 30+ years specialism in estate-agency software. Niche fluency the others lack
  • Award-winning ("Best Large Estate Agency Supplier 2021", Property Academy)
  • Algolia search integration and Strapi 5 Live Preview baked in
  • Modern Jamstack: Next.js + Strapi + Vercel + AWS S3 + Cloudflare
  • 12-month payment plan available for the build cost (cash-flow friendly)
  • Pingdom monitoring, MySQL replication, image processing via Lambda included
!Red Flags
  • 24-month minimum contract, AUTO-RENEWING. The biggest single red flag in any of the four quotes
  • Quote is contingent on Chestertons GLOBAL being built by them first. Forced precedence we did not request
  • Mandatory £669/month support fee = AED 3,100 × 24 months = AED 75K bundled in before we even launch
  • £500/month penalty for delays caused by our own feedback or content delivery
  • Photography (£15/image), copy (£0.35/word), Google Maps charges, live-chat third-party costs all paid by us
  • UK time zone (3-hour offset). Real-time review windows reduced to half a day
  • Total 2-year cash commitment ~AED 153K, plus the auto-renewal beyond
  • Disaster recovery NOT included as standard. Charged separately if requested
  • 60-day proposal expiry. Time-pressure tactic before we have done diligence
Bottom line
Avoid unless we genuinely want a long-term outsourcing relationship locked at the procurement level. The technical work looks competent. The contract structure is the problem. Auto-renewing 24-month commitments belong in telecom contracts, not website builds.
